What is Blockchain?

Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that securely records transactions across a network of computers. Each transaction is stored in a "block" and linked together to form a "chain," ensuring the data cannot be altered without network consensus. While blockchain powers cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum, it is also used in sectors like finance, supply chain management, and healthcare for secure and transparent transactions without intermediaries.

Decentralization

Blockchain operates on a decentralized network, meaning no single entity has control over the entire system. This reduces the risk of corruption and increases resilience against failures.
