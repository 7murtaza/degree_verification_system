What is Blockchain ?

Blockchain is a decentralized digital ledger that securely records transactions across a network of computers. Each transaction is stored in a "block" and linked together to form a "chain," ensuring the data cannot be altered without network consensus. While blockchain powers cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum, it is also used in sectors like finance, supply chain management, Education and healthcare for secure and transparent transactions without intermediaries.

Decentralization

Blockchain operates on a decentralized network, meaning no single entity has control over the entire system. This reduces the risk of corruption and increases resilience against failures.

Transparency

All transactions on a blockchain are recorded on a public ledger that is accessible to all participants in the network. This transparency helps build trust and accountability.

Security

Blockchain uses advanced cryptographic techniques to secure data, ensuring that transactions are safe from tampering and unauthorized access. This makes the blockchain highly secure.

Immutability

Once a transaction is recorded on the blockchain, it cannot be altered or deleted. This immutability ensures the integrity of the data and prevents fraud.
